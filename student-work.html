Pioneer Valley High School  ·  CTE Photography  ·  2025–2026

Magazine Cover Project

I’ve been a photographer long before I was a teacher, and in over two decades behind a lens, I’ve learned that a great image isn’t just about technical skill — it’s about vision. The willingness to see something, and the courage to actually capture it.

What you’re looking at here stopped me cold the first time I saw it.

These five magazine covers were made by first-year photography students — young people experiencing a real camera, real light, and real editorial thinking for the very first time in their lives. And in that first year, they did something that has never been done before in over 20 years of Pioneer Valley High School’s existence: they created work that looks like it belongs on a newsstand.

I teach because I believe in legacy. Not mine — theirs. Every image a student makes is evidence of something they are capable of that they didn’t know before. That evidence doesn’t disappear. It follows them. It builds. It compounds over a lifetime.

Ella, Gregorio, Julio, Davina, Jessica — I am not just proud of what you made. I am proud of the photographers you are becoming. You walked into this program not knowing where to point the camera. You walked out with covers.

That’s not a small thing. That’s everything.

— Chris Silva  |  CTE Photography, Pioneer Valley High School
