CATEGORY 01 / 08
The 7 Elements of Art
TERM 01 / 50
Line
A path made by a moving point. Lines can be straight, curvy, thick, thin, soft, or sharp.
TERM 02 / 50
Shape
A flat area with an edge around it. Two dimensions: width and height. Circle, square, star.
TERM 03 / 50
Form
A shape that looks 3D. Has width, height, and depth. A sphere is a form. A cube is a form.
TERM 04 / 50
Color
What you see when light bounces off something. Color has a name (red, blue), brightness, and strength.
TERM 05 / 50
Value
How light or dark something is. White is lightest. Black is darkest. Everything else is a gray value.
TERM 06 / 50
Texture
How something feels, or how it looks like it would feel. Smooth, rough, bumpy, fuzzy, sharp.
TERM 07 / 50
Space
The room around and between things in an artwork. Positive space is stuff. Negative space is empty.
CATEGORY 02 / 08
Principles of Design
TERM 08 / 50
Balance
When the visual weight of an artwork feels even. Can be symmetrical (both sides match) or asymmetrical (sides differ but still feel balanced).
TERM 09 / 50
Contrast
The difference between two things. Light vs dark, big vs small, smooth vs rough. Contrast makes things pop.
TERM 10 / 50
Visual Hierarchy
The order in which the eye moves around a design. The biggest, boldest stuff is seen first. Less important info is smaller.
TERM 11 / 50
Emphasis
Making one part of a design stand out the most. The focal point. The part your eye lands on first.
TERM 12 / 50
Alignment
Lining up the parts of a design so they look connected and clean. Left-align, center-align, right-align.
TERM 13 / 50
Repetition
Using the same shape, color, or pattern over and over to tie a design together. Repetition builds rhythm.
CATEGORY 03 / 08
Typography
TERM 14 / 50
Typography
The art of using type (letters and words) in a design. Picking fonts, sizing them, and arranging them on the page.
TERM 15 / 50
Typeface
The full design of a set of letters. The shapes, the curves, the style. Arial is a typeface. Helvetica is a typeface.
TERM 16 / 50
Font
A specific size and style of a typeface. Arial 12pt Bold is a font. Arial 18pt Italic is a different font.
TERM 17 / 50
Kerning
The space between two specific letters. Designers adjust kerning so letters look evenly spaced.
TERM 18 / 50
Leading
The space between lines of text, top to bottom. Pronounced “LED-ding” like the metal.
TERM 19 / 50
Tracking
The space between letters across a whole word or sentence. Different from kerning, which is between just two letters.
TERM 20 / 50
Wordmark
A logo made of words only, no symbol. Examples: Google, Coca-Cola, Disney.
TERM 21 / 50
Lockup
The logo art and the wordmark joined as one fixed unit. Always at the same spacing. Never moved around.
CATEGORY 04 / 08
Color & Reproduction
TERM 22 / 50
Hex Code
A 6-character code that locks in an exact color, like #FF6B1A for orange. Same color on every screen and every print.
TERM 23 / 50
CMYK
The four ink colors used in printing: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, blacK. Used on printed boxes, posters, magazines.
TERM 24 / 50
RGB
The three light colors used on screens: Red, Green, Blue. Used on phones, computers, TVs.
TERM 25 / 50
Color Palette
The full set of colors a brand uses. Usually 2 to 5 main colors that always go together. The palette controls the brand feel.
TERM 26 / 50
Warm Colors
Colors that feel hot or sunny. Red, orange, yellow. Often used to feel energetic or fun.
TERM 27 / 50
Cool Colors
Colors that feel calm or chilly. Blue, green, purple. Often used to feel healthy, clean, or trustworthy.
CATEGORY 05 / 08
Branding & Identity
TERM 28 / 50
Logo
The symbol or wordmark that stands for a brand. The visual signature. The Apple apple. The Nike swoosh.
TERM 29 / 50
Brand
The whole personality of a company: how it looks, sounds, and feels to customers. More than just the logo.
TERM 30 / 50
Brand Identity
All the visual pieces of a brand together: logo, colors, fonts, photos, voice. Everything the customer sees and hears.
TERM 31 / 50
Brand DNA
What makes a brand feel like itself. The shapes, colors, and style that stay the same even when the look gets updated.
TERM 32 / 50
Mascot
A character that shows the brand. Tony the Tiger and Cap’n Crunch are mascots. They make a brand easy to remember.
TERM 33 / 50
Tagline
A short phrase that sells the product. Like “They’re great!” or “Just do it.” Short, fun, easy to say.
TERM 34 / 50
Target Audience
The group of people the product is made for. Kids, teens, athletes, busy adults. Every design choice should match the target.
TERM 35 / 50
Brand Refresh
When a designer gives a brand a new look while keeping the heart of the brand. Like updating an old logo without losing what made it famous.
TERM 36 / 50
Creative Brief
A written plan that tells a designer what the client needs. Includes goals, audience, deadline, and budget.
CATEGORY 06 / 08
Packaging & Production
TERM 37 / 50
Mockup
A real-looking picture of your design on a product. A box mockup. A t-shirt mockup. Shows how your design would look in real life.
TERM 38 / 50
Dieline
The flat shape of a box before it gets folded. Shows where to cut, where to fold, and where the panels go.
TERM 39 / 50
Front Panel
The face of a package. The part the shopper sees first. Holds the name, the mascot, and the tagline.
TERM 40 / 50
Print Template
A flat file with all the box panels arranged so they can be printed, cut out, and folded into a real box.
TERM 41 / 50
Proof
A test version of a design before it gets printed or shared. Designers send proofs to clients to check colors, type, and spacing.
TERM 42 / 50
Thumbnail
A tiny rough sketch of a design idea, drawn fast. Used to test lots of ideas before picking the best one.
CATEGORY 07 / 08
Software & Graphics Types
TERM 43 / 50
Adobe Illustrator
The main software designers use to build vector logos, packaging, and illustrations. Industry standard.
TERM 44 / 50
Adobe Photoshop
The main software designers use to edit photos and create raster artwork.
TERM 45 / 50
Vector Graphics
Art made of math (points and lines). Scales to any size without losing quality. Logos are almost always vector.
TERM 46 / 50
Raster Graphics
Art made of tiny squares called pixels. Photos are raster. They lose quality when stretched too big.
TERM 47 / 50
Adobe Firefly
Adobe’s AI tool for generating images from text prompts. Used inside Photoshop and Illustrator.
CATEGORY 08 / 08
Workflow & Process
TERM 48 / 50
Concept Sketch
A by-hand drawing of a design idea before going to the computer. Helps plan the layout.
TERM 49 / 50
Mood Board
A collection of images, colors, and fonts gathered to set the visual mood for a project. Used before designing.
TERM 50 / 50
Reflection
Looking back at finished work to write about what was learned, what went well, and what to change next time.
