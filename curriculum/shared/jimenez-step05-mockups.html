STEP 05 OVERVIEW / 01 / 07
What You Are Building
In Step 04 you built three logo proofs in Adobe Illustrator. Now you pick your personal favorite. That is the logo you will use for this entire step.
Now you are going to show the client how that logo lives in the real world. You will apply it to five branded items using Adobe Illustrator's built-in Mockup tool. You will find your own source product images using Google Images and then use the Mockup tool to wrap your logo onto each item realistically.
This is one of the most impressive parts of any design presentation. Clients respond to mockups because they can finally see the logo the way it will actually look in their business. Your job is to make each mockup look real, clean, and professional.
YOUR FIVE ASSIGNMENTS / 02 / 07
The Five Items You Will Mock Up
Your Illustrator file has five artboards, one for each item below. You will find your own source image for each artboard and use the Mockup tool to apply your logo. Each artboard is a separate deliverable. All five must be complete.
Artboard 1: Vehicle Magnets
Jimenez Mobile Detailing is a mobile business. Their vehicle is their most visible advertisement. Find a blank vehicle door magnet or a car door image and apply the logo so it looks like it would be placed on the side of a real work vehicle. Think about size, proportion, and how the logo would read from across a parking lot.
Artboard 2: Business Card
A business card is the first physical thing a client holds. Find a blank business card mockup, ideally showing the card at a slight angle so it looks dimensional. Apply your logo to the front of the card. Make sure the logo is centered and scaled proportionally. A business card is small, so clarity is everything.
Artboard 3: A-Frame Sign
An A-Frame sign sits on the sidewalk in front of a business or at a job site to attract attention. Find a blank A-Frame or sandwich board sign mockup and apply your logo. The logo should fill the sign panel cleanly and look like it is printed or painted directly on the board. Think about contrast: the logo needs to be readable at a distance.
Artboard 4: Round Hanging Air Freshener
Car air fresheners hang from the mirror and are a classic promotional item for a detailing brand. Find a blank round or custom-shaped hanging air freshener mockup and apply your logo. The logo will need to be scaled small and simplified to fit the shape cleanly. This tests how well your design holds up at a compact size.
Artboard 5: Wild Card Item (Your Choice)
This artboard is yours to choose. Pick a branded item that feels right for Jimenez Mobile Detailing. Good options include a hat, hoodie, t-shirt, work jacket, bucket, foam roller, coffee mug, phone case, or sticker sheet. Pick something you would actually want to own if you were the client. Make it look like real branded merchandise.

FINDING YOUR SOURCE IMAGES / 04 / 07
How to Search for Blank Mockup Product Images
You are not provided source images for this step. Part of the assignment is finding good, clean product images that work well for the Illustrator Mockup tool. Here is how to find them using Google Images.
Step 1: Use the Right Search Terms
Add the word blank or mockup to your search. Use terms like: blank vehicle door magnet mockup, blank business card mockup, blank a-frame sign mockup, blank round air freshener hanging, blank hoodie mockup. Adding the word white or clean background helps find images that are easier to work with.
Step 2: Filter by Image Size
After your search results appear, click Tools (top of the page, below the search bar). Then click Size and choose Large. Larger images give you better detail when you apply them in Illustrator. A small, blurry source image will make your mockup look unprofessional.
Step 3: Choose a Clean, Clear Angle
Pick an image where the product surface is clearly visible, ideally from a slight angle or straight on. Avoid images that are too far away, too dark, or showing the item at an extreme angle. The Illustrator Mockup tool works best when the product surface is clean and well-lit with no distracting background clutter.
Quick Search Reference for Each Item
Vehicle Magnets: blank vehicle door magnet mockup, car door magnet white
Business Card: blank business card mockup white, business card template angle view
A-Frame Sign: blank a-frame sidewalk sign mockup, sandwich board blank white
Air Freshener: blank round hanging air freshener mockup, custom car air freshener blank
Wild Card: blank [item] mockup, example: blank hat mockup front view, blank hoodie mockup flat lay
What to Avoid
Avoid images that already have text or logos on them, images that are heavily watermarked across the main surface, and images where the product is so small you cannot see the surface clearly. Also avoid images with extremely bright or mixed lighting, as the Mockup tool blends using the existing light and shadow of the product, and a bad source image will produce a bad result no matter how good your logo is.
HOW TO COMPLETE STEP 05 / 05 / 07
Step-by-Step Instructions
01. Pick Your Personal Favorite Logo to Mockup
Open your Jimenez_Logo_Refresh.ai file from Step 04. Look at all three proofs you made and pick the one YOU like best. This is your call as the designer. Use that one favorite proof for every artboard in this step. If you are torn between two, go with the one that feels strongest to you.
02. Download and Rename the Mockup Template
Download Jimenez_Mockups.ai from the Project File card above. Rename it immediately by adding your last name. Example: Jimenez_Mockups_Martinez.ai. Save it to your OneDrive project folder. Open it from there. You will see five blank artboards, one for each item in this assignment.
03. Find a Source Image for Each of the Five Items
Before you start mocking up, gather all five source images. Use the Google Image Search tips above to find a clean, large image for each item: vehicle magnet, business card, A-Frame sign, round air freshener, and your wild card item. Save each image to a folder on your desktop or OneDrive. Having all five ready before you start will keep your workflow smooth.
04. Copy Your Approved Logo Into the Template
With both files open, select all the artwork from your favorite artboard in Jimenez_Logo_Refresh.ai. Copy it. Switch to Jimenez_Mockups.ai and paste it onto Artboard 1. Group and resize it as needed. You will reuse this same logo on each artboard. Keep a clean copy grouped and easy to duplicate as you move through each item.
05. Place Your Source Image on the Artboard
Go to File > Place and select your source product image for that artboard. Click once on the artboard to place it. Resize and position the image so the product surface fills the artboard cleanly. You will see an X drawn through the image, which means it is currently a linked file. Stop here. Do not apply the Mockup tool yet. You must embed the image first in the next step.
06. Embed the Placed Image (Required Before Using Mockup Tool)
Placed images are linked by default. A linked image means the .ai file only stores a path to where the image lives on your computer. If the image file moves, gets renamed, or the .ai file is opened on a different computer, the image will appear as a broken red X. Embedding copies the full image data permanently into the .ai file so it can never disconnect, no matter where the file goes.

The Illustrator Mockup tool requires an embedded image to work correctly. Always embed before applying the mockup.

Three ways to embed in the latest Illustrator:
Option A (Control Bar): Select the placed image. Look at the bar across the top of the screen. Click the Embed button that appears there.
Option B (Links Panel): Go to Window > Links. Your image will appear in the Links panel list. Click the three-line menu icon in the top-right corner of the panel and choose Embed Image(s).
Option C (Right-Click): Right-click directly on the placed image on the artboard and choose Embed Image from the menu.

After embedding, the X through the image disappears. The image no longer appears as a link in the Links panel. It is now stored fully inside your file. Do this for every placed image on every artboard before applying the Mockup tool.
07. Apply the Mockup Tool
Copy your favorite logo artwork from Step 04 and paste it onto the artboard. Position the logo on top of the embedded product image roughly where it would appear on the real item. Select both the logo artwork and the embedded image at the same time. Then go to Object > Mockup > Make. Illustrator analyzes the product surface and automatically wraps your logo to its contours, lighting, and shadows. Use the Mockup panel controls to fine-tune scale, position, and opacity until the logo looks like it was actually printed or applied to the product. When it looks right, move on to the next artboard and repeat steps 05, 06, and 07 for each item.
08. Complete All Five Artboards
Work through all five artboards using the same place, embed, and apply sequence. Each artboard should show your logo applied to its product cleanly and realistically. Take your time on each one. The overall quality of this step is judged by how polished the full set looks together, not just one item.
09. Review All Five Mockups Together
Go to View > Fit All in Window to see all five artboards side by side. Check that every mockup looks realistic and professional. The logo should feel like it belongs on each item, not like it was pasted on top. Check scale, contrast, and readability on each one. Also open Window > Links and confirm the Links panel shows no remaining linked files. If any images still appear as linked rather than embedded, embed them now before continuing.
10. Save Your Working Illustrator File to OneDrive
Press Command + S to save. Confirm the file is saving to your OneDrive project folder. You will need this file again for Step 06, the Brand Style Guide. Do not delete it after submitting this step.
11. Export Your PDF (Five Artboards, Five Pages)
Go to File > Save a Copy. Do not use Save As, which would replace your .ai file. In the Format dropdown choose Adobe PDF (.pdf). Name the file Jimenez_Mockups_YourLastName.pdf and click Save.

The Save Adobe PDF dialog will open. Use these settings:

Adobe PDF Preset: Select High Quality Print from the dropdown. This produces a clean, full-resolution PDF that works for both digital grading and printing.
Pages / Range: Set to All. This is the most important setting. It tells Illustrator to include every artboard as a separate page. If you leave it on a single artboard, only one page will export.
Preserve Illustrator Editing Capabilities: Uncheck this box. Leaving it checked embeds the full .ai document data into the PDF, which increases the file size significantly with no benefit for submission.
Marks and Bleeds tab: Leave all printer's marks and bleed settings turned off. These are for commercial printing, not digital submission.
Output tab: Leave at defaults. Color conversion is not needed for this assignment.

Click Save PDF. After the PDF is created, open it in Preview or Adobe Acrobat. Page through all five pages and confirm that every artboard is present, every mockup looks correct, and no images are broken or missing. Do not submit until you have visually verified every page.
12. Upload Your PDF to Canvas
Upload your PDF to this Canvas assignment for grading. Do not upload the .ai file here. Only the PDF goes to Canvas. Keep your .ai file saved in OneDrive. You will use it again in Step 06.

TUS CINCO TAREAS / 02 / 07
Los Cinco Artículos que Vas a Crear en Mockup
Tu archivo de Illustrator tiene cinco mesas de trabajo, una para cada artículo. Tú buscarás tu propia imagen fuente para cada mesa de trabajo y usarás la herramienta Mockup para aplicar tu logo. Cada mesa de trabajo es una tarea separada. Las cinco deben estar completas.
Mesa de Trabajo 1: Imanes para Vehículo
Jimenez Mobile Detailing es un negocio móvil. Su vehículo es su publicidad más visible. Busca un imán para puerta de carro o una imagen de puerta de vehículo en blanco y aplica el logo para que parezca colocado en el lado real de un vehículo de trabajo. Piensa en el tamaño, las proporciones y cómo se leería el logo desde lejos.
Mesa de Trabajo 2: Tarjeta de Presentación
Una tarjeta de presentación es lo primero que el cliente físicamente sostiene. Busca un mockup de tarjeta de presentación en blanco, idealmente mostrando la tarjeta a un ángulo ligero para que se vea con dimensión. Aplica tu logo al frente de la tarjeta. Aségúrate de que el logo esté centrado y a la escala correcta. Una tarjeta de presentación es pequeña, así que la claridad lo es todo.
Mesa de Trabajo 3: Letrero A-Frame
Un letrero A-Frame se coloca en la acera frente a un negocio o en un lugar de trabajo para atraer atención. Busca un mockup de letrero A-Frame o de pizarrón en blanco y aplica tu logo. El logo debe llenar el panel del letrero de forma limpia y parecer que está impreso o pintado directamente en el tablero. Piensa en el contraste: el logo tiene que leerse desde lejos.
Mesa de Trabajo 4: Aromatizante Redondo Colgante
Los aromatizantes de carro cuelgan del espejo y son un artículo promocional clásico para una marca de detailing. Busca un mockup de aromatizante redondo o personalizado en blanco y aplica tu logo. El logo tendrá que escalarse pequeño y simplificarse para que quepa en la forma de manera limpia. Esto pone a prueba qué tan bien funciona tu diseño en tamaño compacto.
Mesa de Trabajo 5: Artículo Wild Card (Tu Elección)
Esta mesa de trabajo es tuya para elegir. Escoge un artículo de marca que encaje bien con Jimenez Mobile Detailing. Buenas opciones incluyen una gorra, sudadera, camiseta, chamarra de trabajo, cubeta, rodillo de espuma, taza de café, funda de teléfono o hoja de stickers. Elige algo que tú mismo querrías tener si fueras el cliente. Házlo ver como mercancía de marca real.

ENCONTRANDO TUS IMÁGENES FUENTE / 04 / 07
Cómo Buscar Imágenes de Productos en Blanco para Mockup
No se te proporcionan imágenes fuente para este paso. Parte de la tarea es encontrar buenas imágenes limpias de productos que funcionen bien con la herramienta Mockup de Illustrator. Aquí te explicamos cómo encontrarlas usando Google Imágenes.
Paso 1: Usa los Términos de Búsqueda Correctos
Agrega la palabra blank o mockup a tu búsqueda. Usa términos como: blank vehicle door magnet mockup, blank business card mockup, blank a-frame sign mockup, blank round air freshener hanging, blank hoodie mockup. Agregar las palabras white o clean background ayuda a encontrar imágenes más fáciles de usar.
Paso 2: Filtra por Tamaño de Imagen
Después de que aparezcan los resultados, haz clic en Herramientas (en la parte superior, debajo de la barra de búsqueda). Luego haz clic en Tamaño y elige Grande. Las imágenes más grandes te dan mejor detalle cuando las aplicas en Illustrator. Una imagen fuente pequeña y borrosa hará que tu mockup se vea poco profesional.
Paso 3: Elige un Ángulo Limpio y Claro
Elige una imagen donde la superficie del producto se vea claramente, de preferencia desde un ángulo leve o de frente. Evita imágenes que estén muy lejos, muy oscuras o que muestren el artículo en un ángulo extremo. La herramienta Mockup de Illustrator funciona mejor cuando la superficie del producto es limpia y bien iluminada sin fondo complicado.
Referencia Rápida de Búsqueda Para Cada Artículo
Imanes para Vehículo: blank vehicle door magnet mockup, car door magnet white
Tarjeta de Presentación: blank business card mockup white, business card template angle view
Letrero A-Frame: blank a-frame sidewalk sign mockup, sandwich board blank white
Aromatizante: blank round hanging air freshener mockup, custom car air freshener blank
Wild Card: blank [artículo] mockup, ejemplo: blank hat mockup front view, blank hoodie mockup flat lay
Qué Evitar
Evita imágenes que ya tengan texto o logos encima, imágenes con marcas de agua en la superficie principal, e imágenes donde el producto es tan pequeño que no puedes ver la superficie con claridad. También evita imágenes con iluminación muy brillante o mezclada, ya que la herramienta Mockup mezcla usando la luz y sombra existente del producto, y una imagen fuente mala producirá un resultado malo sin importar qué tan bueno sea tu logo.

06. Incrusta la Imagen Colocada (Requerido Antes de Usar la Herramienta Mockup)
Las imágenes colocadas son vinculadas de forma predeterminada. Una imagen vinculada significa que el archivo .ai solo guarda una ruta a donde vive la imagen en tu computadora. Si el archivo de imagen se mueve, cambia de nombre, o el .ai se abre en otra computadora, la imagen aparecerá como una X roja rota. Incrustar copia los datos completos de la imagen permanentemente dentro del archivo .ai para que nunca se desconecte.

La herramienta Mockup de Illustrator requiere una imagen incrustada para funcionar correctamente. Siempre incrusta antes de aplicar el mockup.

Tres formas de incrustar en la última versión de Illustrator:
Opción A (Barra de Control): Selecciona la imagen colocada. Mira la barra en la parte superior de la pantalla. Haz clic en el botón Incrustar que aparece ahí.
Opción B (Panel Vínculos): Ve a Ventana > Vínculos. Tu imagen aparecerá en la lista del panel. Haz clic en el icono de menú de tres líneas en la esquina superior derecha del panel y elige Incrustar imagen(es).
Opción C (Clic derecho): Haz clic derecho directamente sobre la imagen colocada en la mesa de trabajo y elige Incrustar imagen del menú.

Después de incrustar, la X sobre la imagen desaparece. La imagen ya no aparece como un vínculo en el panel Vínculos. Ahora está almacenada completamente dentro de tu archivo. Haz esto para cada imagen colocada en cada mesa de trabajo antes de aplicar la herramienta Mockup.

Illustrator analiza la superficie del producto y envuelve automáticamente tu logo a sus contornos, iluminación y sombras. Usa los controles del panel Mockup para ajustar la escala, posición y opacidad hasta que el logo parezca que fue impreso o aplicado realmente al producto. Cuando se vea correcto, pasa a la siguiente mesa de trabajo y repite los pasos 05, 06 y 07 para cada artículo.
