WHAT IS A MAGAZINE COVER / 01 / 06
One Image, One Story
A magazine cover is the front page that makes you want to pick it up. It uses one strong photo and a few bold words to sell the whole magazine in a single glance.
Look at a real cover: a big name at the top (the masthead), one powerful photo behind it, and short headlines that tease the stories inside.
For this project you will design a real-looking agriculture magazine cover that puts the Mark Richardson Center in the spotlight.

WORDS TO KNOW / 04 / 06
Magazine Vocabulary
Learn these six terms. You will use them all the way through the project.
Term 01
Masthead
The magazine’s name and logo at the top of the cover. It is the brand, and usually the biggest text.
Term 02
Cover Image
The one big photo that fills the cover and grabs your eye. It is also called the hero image.
Term 03
Cover Lines
The short, bold headlines that tease the stories inside. They are also called coverlines.
Term 04
Type Hierarchy
Using big, bold type for the main story and smaller type for the rest, so the eye knows where to look first.
Term 05
Bleed
When the photo runs all the way to the edge of the page with no white border. Covers almost always bleed.
Term 06
Photojournalism
Telling a true story with photos taken on location, in real moments, not posed in a studio.

WHAT MAKES A GREAT COVER / 05 / 06
Cover Design Tips
Keep these in mind from your first shot to your final design.
Tip 01
One Strong Subject
Pick one clear subject for your hero photo. A cover with one bold focal point beats a busy one.
Tip 02
Leave Room for Text
Frame your photo with empty space at the top and one side so the masthead and cover lines have a home.
Tip 03
Shoot Vertical
Magazine covers are tall (portrait). Turn the camera vertical when you shoot your hero image.
Tip 04
Keep It Real
This is photojournalism. Real people, real places, and real moments make the strongest covers.
Tip 05
Match the Style
Study your magazine’s real covers and match their fonts, colors, and mood.
