WHAT IS A MAGAZINE COVER / 01 / 07
One Image, One Story
A magazine cover is the front page that makes you want to pick it up. It uses one strong photo and a few bold words to sell the whole magazine in a single glance.
Look at a real cover: a big name at the top (the masthead), one powerful photo behind it, and short headlines that tease the stories inside.
For this project you will design a real-looking agriculture magazine cover featuring Teddy, the MRC security officer and campus photographer named the 2025/2026 Classified Employee of the Year. She is your cover star.

YOUR ASSIGNMENT / 03 / 07
You Are Shooting Teddy
You have been contracted to shoot the cover featuring Teddy. Her photo is your hero image, and the biggest headline on the cover is about her and her honor.
Read her brief below. Pull your main title and subtitle from it. Your smaller cover lines and breakout boxes can tease other things happening on campus: the Ag Farm, the livestock, the Robotics team, student projects.
Teddy
Winner, 2025/2026 Classified Employee of the Year
Teddy is the security officer at the Mark Richardson CTE Center and Ag Farm, but her real focus is people. She practices “relational security”: she learns every student’s name, greets them by it, and makes the whole campus feel seen and safe. Students know her not just as an officer, but as a trusted mentor.
She is also the campus photographer. Her photos of student grit and determination have appeared in school assemblies, school board reports, and local media, building real pride in the MRC.
Teddy goes far beyond her job description. She checks on the animals daily, feeds livestock on weekends, helps the Robotics team prepare late into the night, waters the campus flowers, and pulls weeds, all because she cares. She even became forklift certified to keep students safe in an active CTE environment.
Principal Dr. Paul Robinson calls her watchful and dedicated, and bus drivers, teachers, and students all trust her. She was named the 2025/2026 Classified Employee of the Year, and as her nomination put it, Teddy proves that kindness and heart are the most powerful tools for building a strong school community.

WHAT YOUR COVER MUST HAVE / 04 / 07
Set the Standard
Every finished cover must include all six of these. This is the standard you are graded on.
Must-Have 01
Masthead
The real magazine logo, big and bold at the top.
Must-Have 02
Hero Image
One strong photo of Teddy that bleeds to the edges.
Must-Have 03
Main Title
A bold headline about Teddy and her honor, the biggest text on the cover.
Must-Have 04
Subtitle
A short line under the title that adds detail. This is the deck.
Must-Have 05
Breakout Points
At least three smaller cover lines that tease other MRC stories.
Must-Have 06
Two Breakout Boxes
Two small boxes, each with its own photo and a few words, for extra stories.

WORK AS A TEAM / 05 / 07
Brainstorm Together, Build Your Own
You may work in a group. Slow down and brainstorm together: share ideas, plan your shots, and trade photos and content so everyone has plenty to work with.
But every student turns in their own unique cover. Same group and same shoot, different design. Your layout, your title, and your choices must be your own.
TEAM RULE
Share Ideas, Not Covers
Help each other with ideas, planning, and photos. Then make a cover that is one hundred percent yours.
WORDS TO KNOW / 06 / 07
Magazine Vocabulary
Learn these six terms. You will use them all the way through the project.
Term 01
Masthead
The magazine’s name and logo at the top of the cover. It is the brand, and usually the biggest text.
Term 02
Cover Image
The one big photo that fills the cover and grabs your eye. It is also called the hero image.
Term 03
Cover Lines
The short, bold headlines that tease the stories inside. They are also called coverlines.
Term 04
Type Hierarchy
Using big, bold type for the main story and smaller type for the rest, so the eye knows where to look first.
Term 05
Bleed
When the photo runs all the way to the edge of the page with no white border. Covers almost always bleed.
Term 06
Photojournalism
Telling a true story with photos taken on location, in real moments, not posed in a studio.

YOUR MISSION / 07 / 07
What You Will Do
You will work like a real photo editor in four steps: plan, capture, design, and reflect.
STEP 01
Plan
Read Teddy’s brief, plan your shots, and sketch your cover layout.
STEP 02
Capture
Shoot your hero photo and two breakout photos, then turn in a RAW folder screenshot.
STEP 03
Design
Build the cover in Photoshop with the masthead, your words, and two breakout boxes.
STEP 04
Reflect
Answer four questions about your cover and the choices you made.
