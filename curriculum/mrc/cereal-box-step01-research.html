Cereal Box Research
Pick a real cereal. Study the box. Find out who it sells to.
MARKET RESEARCH / 01 / 06
Why Real Designers Start Here
Before you draw a single line, you study what already works. Real designers do this on every project. They look at what is on the shelf and ask why it sells.
In this step, you and your partner pick one real cereal in your category. You study how its box is designed. You figure out who it is made for and how the design helps it sell.
Everything you learn here goes into your own brand later.

WHAT THE DOC ASKS YOU / 03 / 06
The Eight Research Questions
Scroll through and you will see exactly what the Word doc is going to ask. Answer each one with full sentences.
Q1: Cereal Name
What is the full name of the cereal you picked? Use the exact wording from the box.
Q2: Photo of the Front
Paste a clear photo of the front of the box. The words need to be easy to read.
Q3: Official Website
Find the brand’s official website. Paste the full link in the doc.
Q4: Target Audience
Who is the cereal trying to sell to? Kids, parents, athletes, busy adults. Explain why you think so.
Q5: Which 7 Elements?
Which Elements of Art does the box use? It may not use all seven. Name the ones you see and where you see them.
Q6: Two Competitors
Find two other cereals that compete with yours. Name them and say what they have in common.
Q7: How Design Sells
Write a short answer about how the box’s design helps the cereal sell. Use art words like contrast, typography, and layout.
Q8: Your Hot Take
Would you buy it? Why or why not? What would you change to make it better?
TIPS FOR STRONG ANSWERS / 04 / 06
How to Score Higher
Complete Sentences
No one-word answers. Every answer is a full sentence or two.
Be Specific
Point at exact words, colors, or pictures on the box. Generic comments lose points.
Use Art Words
Drop in words like contrast, typography, layout, and hierarchy.
Connect Back
Always tie your answer back to the shopper the cereal is trying to reach.
