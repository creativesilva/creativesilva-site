Camera Modes  •  Step 1
Capture Eight Photos
Four in AV, four in TV.
YOUR EIGHT SHOTS / 01 / 05
What to Capture
You and your partner will capture eight photos around the MRC campus: four in AV mode and four in TV mode.
Keep your ISO on Auto the whole time. That lets the camera handle brightness while you focus on your one setting.
SET UP THE CAMERA / 02 / 05
Before You Shoot
Get the camera ready before each set of shots. Scroll through each setting.
Setup 01
Shoot RAW
Set image quality to RAW so you have room to edit later. On the R50 these are .CR3 files.
Setup 02
ISO on Auto
Set ISO to Auto in the menu and leave it there. The camera handles brightness for you.
Setup 03
Pick Your Lens
For AV depth-of-field shots use the 50mm lens. For TV motion shots the 18-45mm kit lens is easiest, but the 50mm or the 55-210mm telephoto work too.
Setup 04
Turn the Dial
Turn the mode dial to AV or TV. The screen shows the setting you control. Spin the top dial to change its number.

AV SHOTS / 03 / 05
Control Your Focus
Put on the 50mm lens and turn the dial to AV. Spin the top dial to change the f-number. Take four AV photos: two with a blurry background and two with everything sharp.
AV: Shallow
Blurry Background (take 2)
Use a low f-number (f/1.8 to f/4). Get close to your subject so the background melts into a soft blur. Try a portrait or a close-up detail.
AV: Deep
All in Focus (take 2)
Use a high f-number (f/11 or more). Find a scene with depth and layers so the front and the back are both sharp.

TV SHOTS / 04 / 05
Control the Motion
Turn the dial to TV. The 18-45mm kit lens is easiest here, but the 50mm or the 55-210mm telephoto work too. Take four TV photos: two that freeze motion and two that blur it.
TV: Freeze
Freeze the Action (take 2)
Use a fast shutter (1/500 or faster). Catch something quick: a jump, a run, a kick. The motion looks frozen and sharp.
TV: Blur
Blur the Motion (take 2)
Use a slow shutter (1/30 or slower). Hold steady and let a moving subject streak: a walking student, a bike, a waving flag.
