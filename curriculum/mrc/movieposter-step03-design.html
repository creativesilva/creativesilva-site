Movie Poster  •  Step 3
Build Your Poster
Design it like the real thing.
SET UP YOUR POSTER / 01 / 06
Start a New File
Open Photoshop. Go to File > New and make a tall (portrait) poster, about 11 by 17 inches, RGB color, 300 ppi.
Place your protagonist as the main image and let it bleed to the edges. Bring in your antagonist (villain or monster) too, looming or in shadow, so both are clear at a glance. Darken the background so it feels moody, and save as a .PSD in your project folder.
GET YOUR FONTS / 02 / 06
Find and Install a Font
Your title needs the right font. You have two places to get one: dafont.com (free downloads) and Adobe Fonts (built into your Adobe account). Here is how to use each.
Option A: dafont.com, download and install:
daFont 01
Browse and Download
Go to dafont.com, find a font you like (try the Horror or Gothic categories), and click Download. A .zip saves to your Downloads.
daFont 02
Unzip the File
In Finder, open Downloads and double-click the .zip to unzip it. Inside is a font file that ends in .ttf or .otf.
daFont 03
Install the Font
Double-click the font file. Font Book opens with a preview. Click Install Font. The font is now on the Mac.
daFont 04
Use It in Photoshop
If Photoshop was open, quit and reopen it so it sees the new font. Pick the Type tool and find your font in the font menu.

Option B: Adobe Fonts, activate with no download:
Adobe 01
Open Adobe Fonts
Go to fonts.adobe.com and sign in with your school Adobe account, the same one you use for Photoshop.
Adobe 02
Find a Font
Browse or search for a font that fits your scary mood. Use the filters for a bold or display style.
Adobe 03
Activate It
Open the font family and turn on the Add font toggle. Adobe installs it for you, with no download.
Adobe 04
Use It in Photoshop
Back in Photoshop, pick the Type tool and find your activated font in the font menu. It shows a small cloud icon.

TIP
Match the Mood
Scary movies love rough, sharp, or hand-drawn fonts. Pick one that feels like your story, then make it big in the next step.
DESIGN THE TITLE TREATMENT / 03 / 06
Make the Title
Your movie title is the star. Using the font you just installed, make it big and bold. You decide where it lives on the poster, use what you learned from the real posters you studied.
Add a soft glow, a rough edge, or a shadow so the title stands out against the dark image.
ADD YOUR WORDS / 04 / 06
Tagline, Credits, and Rating
Use the Type tool to add the rest of the words. Scroll through each pointer.
Words 01
Tagline and Subtitle
Add your one creepy tagline and a short subtitle. Keep both short and easy to read. You decide where they go.
Words 02
Actor Names
Add 2 to 3 actor names. Decide which names are biggest and where they belong, the way you saw on the real posters you studied.
Words 03
Production Logo
Add your made-up production company logo or name. You choose how big it is and where it sits.
Words 04
Billing and Rating
Add a thin billing block (cast, director, studio) plus the PG-13 rating and a release line.
Words 05
Use Type Hierarchy
This is the real challenge: make the most important words biggest and the least important smallest. Size and placement tell the eye what matters. The order is your call.
Words 06
Keep It Readable
Dark posters can hide text. Add a soft glow or shadow so every word still pops.

SET THE MOOD / 05 / 06
Turn Day into Night
► Watch on YouTube
You shot in the daytime, so now make it night. First swap the bright sky: go to Edit > Sky Replacement and pick a dark, stormy, or night sky. This is the biggest step from day to night.
Then grade the whole poster with the Camera Raw Filter (Filter > Camera Raw Filter): lower the brightness, cool the colors toward blue, deepen the shadows, and add a vignette around the edges.
For a fast color grade, watch the one-minute tutorial (top right) on turning day into night with LUT profiles (Edit > Color Lookup).
Blend in your supporting images for fog or texture. The whole poster should feel cold and tense, like a real horror one-sheet.
SAVE AND SUBMIT / 06 / 06
Save as JPG and Submit
When your poster is done, flatten it with Layer > Flatten Image. Keep a PSD only if you want one.
Turn in a JPG, not a PSD. Use File > Save a Copy, choose JPG, full size. Name it:
FirstName LastInitial - Movie Poster.jpg
Submit your finished poster JPG to Step 3 in Canvas.
