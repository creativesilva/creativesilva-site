TWO CAMERA MODES / 01 / 05
Take the Wheel
Until now, you shot in Auto and the camera made every choice for you. Now you take the wheel with two new modes on your Canon EOS R50: TV and AV.
These are semi-manual modes. You make one important choice, and the camera figures out the rest. They are the easiest way to start controlling how your photos look.
THE EXPOSURE TRIANGLE / 02 / 05
Three Things Make a Photo Bright
Every photo gets its brightness from three things working together. Photographers call this the exposure triangle.
Part 01
Aperture
How wide the lens opens to let light in. It also controls how much of the photo is in focus.
Part 02
Shutter Speed
How long the lens stays open. It controls whether motion looks frozen or blurry.
Part 03
ISO
How sensitive the camera is to light. Keep this on Auto and let the camera handle it.

In TV and AV you control one of these, and the camera balances the other two. With ISO on Auto, you only think about one setting at a time.
AV MODE / 03 / 05
AV: You Control Focus
AV means Aperture Priority. You pick the aperture (the f-number), and the camera picks the shutter speed for you.
Aperture controls depth of field, how much of your photo is sharp. A low f-number like f/1.8 blurs the background, perfect for a portrait. A high f-number like f/11 keeps everything sharp, perfect for a wide scene.
Use AV when focus matters most: portraits, close-up details, or scenes with lots of depth.
TV MODE / 04 / 05
TV: You Control Motion
TV means Shutter Priority. You pick the shutter speed, and the camera picks the aperture for you.
Shutter speed controls motion. A fast shutter like 1/500 freezes action razor sharp. A slow shutter like 1/30 lets moving things blur into streaks.
Use TV when movement matters most: sports, running, bikes, flowing water, or anything in motion.
YOUR MISSION / 05 / 05
What You Will Do
You will shoot, edit, and reflect, just like a real photographer. Three steps, three turn-ins.
STEP 01

STEP 02

STEP 03
