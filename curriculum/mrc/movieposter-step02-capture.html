Shoot Your Scene
Capture your moody hero image.
SHOOT YOUR SCENE / 01 / 05
Shoot by Day, Go Dark Later
Go to the spot you scouted: the field, the barn, or the shop. Build fear with light, shadow, and angle, not action.
We cannot be on campus at night, so you will shoot in the daytime and turn it into night later in Photoshop.
SHOOT VERTICAL
Hold the Camera Tall (Portrait)
Your poster is a tall, vertical shape, so every photo must be shot in portrait orientation. Turn the camera sideways so the frame is taller than it is wide. A wide (landscape) photo will not fill the poster and leaves empty gaps. If your shot is wider than it is tall, turn the camera and take it again.
REQUIRED
Light It With the Off-Camera Flash
You must light your shot with the off-camera flash (the Godox AD200 on a stand, triggered from your Canon R50), not just natural light. The flash lets you shape the look and mood: light from one side, from below, or rim-light your subject from behind. Watch the lighting video in the overview first.
SHOOT FOR NIGHT
Make the Convert Easy
Shoot on an overcast day or in open shade so the light is soft and even. Keep some sky in your frame so you can replace it, and keep harsh sun off your subject. Soft daytime photos turn into night the cleanest.
SHOOT YOUR PROTAGONIST / 02 / 05
Frame the Hero
Start with your protagonist, the star of the film. This hero shot carries the whole poster. Scroll through each pointer as you shoot.
Shot 01
Shoot Vertical
Turn the camera tall (portrait). Posters are tall, so your hero photo should be too.
Shot 02
Use Dramatic Light
Light from one side, from below, or with a flashlight. Hard shadows feel scary.
Shot 03
Strong Expression
Direct your character: a stare, a look back, fear in the eyes. Mood is everything.
Shot 04
Leave Title Room
Keep the top or bottom a little open so your title and tagline will fit later.
Shot 05
Shoot a Lot
Try many angles, low and high. Creepy angles make a creepy poster.

SHOOT YOUR ANTAGONIST / 03 / 05
Capture the Threat
Now capture your antagonist, the villain or scary monster, so it reads as a clear threat. Keep it PG-13: scary, not gross.
Ideas: a second actor in deep shadow, a silhouette in a doorway, a figure in a mask, a pair of eyes, or a looming shape you will darken later. Often the villain looms large or hides in the dark.
Also grab two or more atmosphere shots: fog, the empty location, a creepy detail, or a texture, to blend in when you design.
ORGANIZE YOUR FILES / 04 / 05
Keep It Organized
Use the project folder you downloaded from the overview. If you have not yet, move it into your OneDrive Digital Arts folder.
Offload all your RAW files into the RAW Images subfolder inside it. Keep every part of this project organized here.
TURN IT IN / 05 / 05
Screenshot Your RAW Folder
Open Finder and switch to Icon view so you can see the photo thumbnails.
Open your RAW Images folder so the window shows the folder name and your thumbnails inside. Press the F15 key to take the screenshot. On our Macs, F15 saves it straight to your Desktop as a PNG.
