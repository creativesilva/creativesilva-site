WHAT IS A MOVIE POSTER / 01 / 07
One Image, One Scare
A movie poster is one image that makes you want to see the film. It uses one strong photo, a title, and a short tagline to sell the whole story in a single glance.
A scary movie poster does it with mood: dark shadows, cold color, and a feeling that something is wrong.
For this project you will design a real-looking movie poster for a PG-13 scary movie set at the Mark Richardson Center.
PICK YOUR CONCEPT / 02 / 07
Choose a Scary Story
Pick one of these three concepts, or pitch your own PG-13 idea. Each one is set right here at the MRC.
Concept 01
The Cornfield
A student cuts through the MRC cornfield at dusk and the rows close behind them. The maze keeps changing, and they are not alone.
Tagline: “You can’t find your way out.”
Concept 02
Night Shift
A student locks up the ag-mechanics shop alone after dark. The machines power on by themselves, and a shadow moves in the sparks.
Tagline: “Some machines never clock out.”
Concept 03
The Scarecrow
The field scarecrow disappears each morning and turns up a little closer to campus each night. Nobody believes it, until it is at the door.
Tagline: “It only moves when no one is looking.”

KEEP IT PG-13 / 03 / 07
Scary, Not Gross
Scary is great. Gross is not. Your movie must stay PG-13 and school-appropriate. Build fear with mood, shadow, and suspense, not blood, gore, or weapons.
Think about the scariest part of a movie, the part before anything even happens: the dark hallway, the sound behind you, the thing you cannot quite see. That is your goal.
THE RULE
Get It Approved
Mr. Silva must approve your concept before you shoot. No blood, no gore, no weapons, nothing that breaks school rules. When in doubt, ask.
WHAT YOUR POSTER MUST HAVE / 04 / 07
Set the Standard
Every finished poster must include all six of these. This is the standard you are graded on.
Must-Have 01
Title Treatment
Your movie title, big and styled to match the scary mood.
Must-Have 02
Tagline
One short, creepy line that hooks the viewer.
Must-Have 03
Hero Image
One strong, moody photo of your character or scene that fills the poster.
Must-Have 04
Billing Block
A credits block at the bottom: cast, director, and studio.
Must-Have 05
Rating and Date
The PG-13 rating and a release line, like “In theaters this fall.”
Must-Have 06
Dark Mood
A moody color grade with deep shadows. It must feel scary, not bright.

WORK AS A TEAM / 05 / 07
Brainstorm Together, Build Your Own
You may work in a group. Slow down and brainstorm together: share concept ideas, act in each other’s shoots, and trade photos so everyone has plenty to work with.
But every student turns in their own unique poster. Same group and same shoot, different design. Your title, your mood, and your choices must be your own.
TEAM RULE
Share Ideas, Not Posters
Help each other with ideas, acting, and photos. Then make a poster that is one hundred percent yours.
WORDS TO KNOW / 06 / 07
Movie Poster Vocabulary
Learn these six terms. You will use them all the way through the project.
Term 01
Title Treatment
The styled design of the movie’s name. The font and look set the mood before you read a word.
Term 02
Tagline
One short, haunting line that hooks you, like “You can’t find your way out.”
Term 03
Key Art
The main image of the poster, also called the hero image. It carries the whole feeling of the movie.
Term 04
Billing Block
The thin block of credits at the bottom: the cast, the director, and the studio.
Term 05
Mood and Tone
The feeling the poster gives, set with color, light, and shadow. Scary posters are dark and moody.
Term 06
Teaser vs Theatrical
A teaser poster shows just enough to spark curiosity. A theatrical poster shows more right before release.

YOUR MISSION / 07 / 07
What You Will Do
You will work like a real movie marketing artist in four steps: plan, capture, design, and reflect.
STEP 01
Plan
Pick or pitch a PG-13 scary concept, cast it, and sketch your poster.
STEP 02
Capture
Shoot your moody hero image and supporting shots, then turn in a RAW folder screenshot.
STEP 03
Design
Build the poster with a title treatment, a tagline, and a billing block.
STEP 04
Reflect
Answer four questions about your poster and the choices you made.
