WHAT IS A PANORAMA / 01 / 04
One Photo, Wide as the World
A panorama is a very wide photo made by joining several photos into one. It captures far more than a single shot can: a whole landscape, a long hallway, or a city skyline.
You take a row of photos that overlap, then the computer stitches them edge to edge into one wide image. The result tells a visual story across movement and space.
THE TWO PARTS / 02 / 04
Capture, Then Merge
This is a two-part project. In Part A (Step 1) you photograph RAW images for two panoramas with a camera and tripod. In Part B (Step 2) you stitch and edit them in Photoshop. Then you finish with a short reflection.
You will make at least two panoramas, each built from 4 to 5 photos.

SHOOTING IN RAW / 03 / 04
Why RAW This Time
This time you shoot in RAW, not JPEG. A RAW file keeps all the data the camera sensor captured, so you can fix exposure and color later with no loss in quality. On our Canon cameras, RAW files end in .CR3.
RAW files are larger and need editing software to open and merge. That is exactly what Photoshop is for in Part B.
YOUR MISSION / 04 / 04
What You Will Do
You will work like a real photographer: capture, merge, and reflect. Three steps, three turn-ins.
STEP 01
Capture
Shoot RAW images for two panoramas with a camera and tripod, then turn in a screenshot of your RAW folder.
STEP 02
Merge & Edit
Stitch each set of photos into one wide panorama in Photoshop, then submit two finished JPGs.
STEP 03
Reflect
Answer four short questions about how your panorama project went.
