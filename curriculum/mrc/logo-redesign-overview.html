Logo Redesign

WHY LOGOS MATTER / 01 / 04
A Logo Is a Brand’s Face
A logo is the face of a brand. It is one small mark that stands for a whole company: its products, its style, and its promise to you.
You already recognize hundreds of logos in a split second, even with no name attached. A checkmark. A bitten apple. A pair of golden arches. That fast recognition is the whole job of a logo.
The best logos are simple enough to draw from memory. That is not an accident. It is the result of careful design.
SIMPLE WINS / 02 / 04
The More Successful, the Simpler
Look at how the Starbucks logo changed over time. The first version in 1971 had a detailed siren, a brown color, and the words “Starbucks Coffee” wrapped around a ring. Each redesign removed something: the outer text, then the ring, until only a clean green siren was left.
This happens to almost every big brand. As a company grows, its logo gets simpler, not fancier. Nike dropped its name and kept the swoosh. Apple dropped the rainbow stripes and the word “Apple.” Shell, McDonald’s, and Mastercard all did the same.
Why? A simple logo works everywhere: tiny on a phone screen, huge on a billboard, stamped on a cup, or stitched on a shirt. It is easy to remember and hard to mess up. Simple is not lazy. Simple is powerful.
THE BIG IDEA / 03 / 04
Less, but Better
Great designers follow a few rules to keep a logo strong. Keep these in mind for your own redesign.
Principle 01
Simple
Few shapes, few colors. If you can draw it from memory, it is working.
Principle 02
Memorable
One strong idea that sticks in your head after a single look.
Principle 03
Scalable
Looks sharp tiny on a phone or huge on a billboard, on screen or on a shirt.
Principle 04
Timeless
Avoids trendy tricks that will look old and dated in a year.
Principle 05
One Color
A great logo still reads clearly in plain black or plain white.

YOUR MISSION / 04 / 04
What You Will Do
You will take a famous logo you already know and reimagine it. You will work like a real designer: sketch first, get feedback, then build a polished digital version. Three steps, three turn-ins.
STEP 01
Thumbnails
Sketch three quick redesign ideas for a famous logo, then bring them to Mr. Silva for a check.
STEP 02
Final Sketch
Use the feedback to draw one polished final sketch, photograph your work, and turn it in.
STEP 03
Digital Redesign
Rebuild your new logo in Photoshop at 2000 x 2000 pixels and turn in the final JPG.
