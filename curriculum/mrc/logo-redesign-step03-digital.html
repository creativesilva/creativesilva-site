Logo Redesign  •  Step 3
Rebuild It in Photoshop
Turn your sketch into a clean digital logo.
SET UP YOUR DOCUMENT / 01 / 04
Start a New File
Open Photoshop. Go to File > New. Make a square artboard, 2000 × 2000 pixels, RGB color. In the Name box, name it FirstName LastInitial - Logo Redesign, then click Create.
Save right away. Go to File > Save As and save the layered .PSD file into your OneDrive Digital Arts folder. Save often as you work.

BUILD YOUR LOGO / 02 / 04
Tools to Use
Rebuild your final sketch as a clean digital logo. Lead with the Pen tool, then use whatever else you need. Scroll for pointers on each tool.
Pen Tool (Use This)
Pen Tool First
The Pen tool makes clean, sharp paths you fully control. Click to drop anchor points and drag to make curves. It is the pro way to build a logo and it stays crisp at any size. Use it as much as you can.
Shape Tools
Build With Shapes
The Rectangle, Ellipse, Polygon, and Line tools make crisp shapes fast. Hold Shift while you drag to keep them even, like a perfect circle or square.
Layers
Work in Layers
Put each part of your logo on its own layer and name it. You can hide, move, recolor, or restack a part without touching the rest.
Selections
Make Selections
Use the Marquee (boxes and ovals), the Lasso (freehand), or Object Selection to grab an area, then fill it, move it, or delete it.
Color
Fill With Color
Set your foreground color, then fill a shape or selection with Edit > Fill or the Paint Bucket. Keep to two or three strong colors.
Eyedropper
Grab Colors
Click the Eyedropper, then click a color in your reference to pick it up. This keeps your colors consistent and on theme.
Align
Center It Up
Use the Move tool and the Align buttons to center your logo on the artboard so it looks balanced and clean.
Keep It Simple
Simple and Bold
Strong shapes, few colors, high contrast. Your logo should read in one second, even when it is shrunk down small.

ADD TYPE AND FONTS / 03 / 04
Find the Right Font
Many logos include the brand name or a few letters. Choose a font that matches your new style: bold and clean, fun, sharp, or retro. Use one or two fonts at most.
You can get fonts from two places. Pick one and add the font you like.
Source 01
dafont.com
A huge library of free fonts you can download. Browse by style, then download the font file to the computer.
Source 02
Adobe Fonts
Hundreds of quality fonts built right into your Adobe account. Turn one on and it shows up in Photoshop, no download needed.
« drag or swipe for more »
A downloaded font has to be installed on the computer before Photoshop can use it. Mr. Silva will demo how to install a font in class.
Use the Type tool to add your text. Keep it readable and let it match the shapes in your logo.
SAVE AND SUBMIT / 04 / 04
Save Twice, Submit the JPG
Save your layered Photoshop file (.PSD) one more time to your OneDrive Digital Arts folder so you keep all your layers.
Then make a flat image to turn in. Use File > Save a Copy and choose JPG. Name the JPG:
FirstName LastInitial - Logo Redesign.jpg
Submit the JPG to Step 3 in Canvas. That is your finished, reimagined logo.
