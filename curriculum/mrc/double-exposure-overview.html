WHAT IS A DOUBLE EXPOSURE / 01 / 04
Two Photos, One Image
A double exposure blends two photos into a single image. The classic look is a portrait or silhouette filled with a second picture: a forest, a city, clouds, or water. It is one of the fastest ways to make something that looks like real art.
The secret is blending modes: a Photoshop setting that changes how one layer mixes with the layer underneath it. In Step 1 you will photograph a portrait in RAW, process it, cut out your subject, and fuse it with a second image.

WORDS TO KNOW / 03 / 04
Double Exposure Vocabulary
Learn these eight terms. You will use them through the whole exercise.
Vocabulary
Double Exposure
Two images blended into one. The look comes from old film cameras exposing a single frame twice.
Vocabulary
Blending Mode
A layer setting that controls how a layer mixes with the one below it.
Vocabulary
Layer Mask
A black-and-white attachment that hides or shows parts of a layer. Black hides, white shows.
Vocabulary
Select and Mask
The Photoshop workspace for making and refining a selection, especially around hair and edges.
Vocabulary
Opacity
How see-through a layer is, from 0% (invisible) to 100% (solid).
Vocabulary
Silhouette
A subject shown as a solid dark shape against a lighter background.
Vocabulary
RAW (CR3)
An unprocessed image straight off the camera sensor. Canon’s version is the .CR3. It holds far more editing data than a JPG.
Vocabulary
Camera Raw
The window that opens when you open a RAW file, where you set exposure, color, and contrast before Photoshop.

YOUR MISSION / 04 / 04
What You Will Do
This is a one-step exercise. Everything happens in Step 1: Capture, Edit, and Create. Here is the plan at a glance.
The Plan
Capture
Photograph a portrait in RAW against a simple background, and get a second image to blend in.
The Plan
Process
Open the RAW file in Camera Raw and lock in your exposure and color.
The Plan
Cut Out
Mask your subject off the background with Select and Mask.
The Plan
Blend and Export
Stack the second image, use blending modes to fuse them, then export 2 to 3 final JPGs.
