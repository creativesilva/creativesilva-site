Plan Your Poster

PICK YOUR CONCEPT / 01 / 05
Choose or Pitch
Use one of the three ideas from the overview as a starting point, or pitch your own creative concept to Mr. Silva for approval. Then write your logline: one sentence that says who the story is about, where it happens, and what is wrong.
KEEP IT PG-13
Get It Approved First
Your concept must be school-appropriate and approved by Mr. Silva before you shoot. Scary with mood and suspense, never with blood, gore, or weapons.
RESEARCH THE GENRE / 02 / 05
Study Real Scary Posters
Before you design anything, study how real scary movie posters work. As a group, pull up posters from horror and thriller films and notice what they share. Spend real time on this, good research leads to good design.
Look At 01
Color Schemes
What colors set the mood? Notice the black, cold blue, deep red, or sickly green in horror posters.
Look At 02
Font Choices
Are the titles sharp, rough, hand-drawn, or clean? How does the lettering make you feel?
Look At 03
Layout
Where do the title, the faces, and the credits sit? How is the empty space used?
Look At 04
Hero and Villain
How do they show the good guy and the threat? The villain often looms large or hides in shadow.
Look At 05
Genre Trends
What do recent scary-movie posters have in common? Find ideas you can borrow.

TEAM RESEARCH
Decide Together
Talk through what you find and agree as a group on a color scheme, a font style, and a layout direction before you build. Then each of you makes your own poster from that shared plan.
WRITE AND CAST / 03 / 05
Build Your Movie
Now decide the pieces of your movie. These choices give you the people, the words, and the look for your poster. Scroll through each one.
Decide 01
Protagonist
Who is the hero, the star we follow? Cast a friend to play them.
Decide 02
Antagonist
Who or what is the villain or monster? How will you show the threat?
Decide 03
Title
Name your movie. Short and creepy works best.
Decide 04
Tagline
Write one haunting line that hooks the viewer.
Decide 05
Subtitle
A short second line that adds detail or sets up the story.
Decide 06
Studio and Cast
Make up a production company name and pick two or three actor names for the credits.
Decide 07
Location and Mood
Pick a moody spot and your colors: cold blues, deep shadows, low fog.

SKETCH YOUR LAYOUT / 04 / 05
Plan the Poster
On paper, sketch a quick thumbnail of your poster. Use design hierarchy: plan where each piece goes and how big it is.
Show your protagonist as the main focus and your antagonist (villain or monster) as a clear threat. Put the big title near the bottom, mark spots for the tagline and subtitle, the production logo, and the actor names and credits.
Leave room for the text. Both characters and the title should read at a glance.
GET IT CHECKED / 05 / 05
Get Approved
Before you shoot, show Mr. Silva your concept, your cast and location, and your layout sketch. This is a required checkpoint.
CHECKPOINT
Approval First, Shoot Second
Your concept must be approved so it stays PG-13 and school-safe. Get the green light, then grab a camera for Step 2.
