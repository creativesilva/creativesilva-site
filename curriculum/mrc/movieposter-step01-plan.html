Movie Poster  •  Step 1
Plan Your Poster
Pick your story and sketch the layout.
PICK YOUR CONCEPT / 01 / 04
Choose or Pitch
Choose one of the three concepts from the overview, or pitch your own PG-13 idea. Then write your logline: one sentence that says who the story is about, where it happens, and what is wrong.
KEEP IT PG-13
Get It Approved First
Your concept must be school-appropriate and approved by Mr. Silva before you shoot. Scary with mood and suspense, never with blood, gore, or weapons.
WRITE AND CAST / 02 / 04
Build Your Movie
Now decide the pieces of your movie. These choices give you the words and the look for your poster. Scroll through each one.
Decide 01
Title
Name your movie. Short and creepy works best.
Decide 02
Tagline
Write one haunting line that hooks the viewer.
Decide 03
Character
Cast a friend as your lead. Who is the story about?
Decide 04
Location
Pick a moody spot: the field, the barn, or the shop, after dark.
Decide 05
Mood
Decide your feeling and colors: cold blues, deep shadows, low fog.

SKETCH YOUR LAYOUT / 03 / 04
Plan the Poster
On paper, sketch a quick thumbnail of your poster. Plan where each piece goes:
Block out your hero image, put the big title near the bottom, mark a spot for the tagline, and leave a thin strip at the very bottom for the billing block.
Leave room for the title. Do not plan to bury it in a busy part of the photo.
GET IT CHECKED / 04 / 04
Get Approved
Before you shoot, show Mr. Silva your concept, your cast and location, and your layout sketch. This is a required checkpoint.
CHECKPOINT
Approval First, Shoot Second
Your concept must be approved so it stays PG-13 and school-safe. Get the green light, then grab a camera for Step 2.
