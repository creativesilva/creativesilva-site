Panorama Project  •  Step 1
Capture Your RAW Images
Camera, tripod, and RAW files.
CHOOSE TWO LOCATIONS / 01 / 05
Pick Two Scenes
Choose 2 locations, indoors or outdoors. Look for interesting textures, structures, or open space. Pick scenes that are wide and tell a story across the frame.
You will shoot two different panoramas, each 4 to 5 photos.
SET UP YOUR CAMERA / 02 / 05
Camera and Tripod Setup
Get your gear ready before you shoot. Scroll through each setup step.
Setup 01
Format the SD Card
Erase old photos first so you have room and start clean. Format the card in the camera menu.
Setup 02
Set Quality to RAW
In the menu, set image quality to RAW only, no JPEG. On our Canon cameras this makes .CR3 files.
Setup 03
Mount the Tripod
Put the camera on a tripod and level it. A steady, level camera is the key to a clean stitch.
Setup 04
Switch to AV Mode
Use AV (aperture priority). Pick your aperture: a low f-stop like f/4 blurs the background; a high f-stop like f/11 keeps more in focus.
Setup 05
Set Your Exposure
Check the light meter. The photo should not be too dark or too bright. Take a test shot and adjust.

SHOOT THE PANORAMA / 03 / 05
Pan Slow and Overlap
Now capture your photos. Move across the scene in even, overlapping steps.
Focus pro tip: if the camera will not lock focus, flip the AF / MF switch to MF (manual focus). That turns on the focus ring on the lens so you can twist it to set focus by hand. Switch it back to AF when you finish, for the next student.
Shoot 01
Take 4 to 5 Photos
Shoot 4 to 5 RAW photos for one panorama, moving across the scene from one side to the other.
Shoot 02
Pan Slow and Level
Turn slowly in a level, horizontal motion. Keep the camera flat the whole way across.
Shoot 03
Overlap Each Shot
Overlap each photo with the one before it by 15 to 20 percent so the computer can match them up.
Shoot 04
Lock Focus (Optional)
For a blurry background, turn off autofocus after your first shot so focus stays the same. Turn autofocus back on when you finish, for the next student.
Shoot 05
Do It Again
Move to a different spot and shoot your second panorama the same way. Two panoramas total.

ORGANIZE YOUR FILES / 04 / 05
Keep It Organized
Use the Panorama folder you downloaded from the overview. If you have not yet, move it into your OneDrive Photography folder.
Offload all your .CR3 RAW files into the RAW Images subfolder inside it. Keep every part of this project organized here from start to finish, including your reflection at the end.
TURN IT IN / 05 / 05
Screenshot Your RAW Folder
Open Finder and switch to Icon view so you can see the photo thumbnails.
Open your RAW Images folder. Make sure the window shows the folder name RAW Images at the top and your image thumbnails inside. No contact sheet, just the folder.
Press the F15 key to take the screenshot. On our Macs, F15 saves the screenshot straight to your Desktop as a PNG.
Find it on the Desktop and rename it:
FirstName LastInitial - Panorama (PNG)
