WHAT IS A DOUBLE EXPOSURE / 01 / 04
Two Photos, One Image
A double exposure blends two photos into a single image. The classic look is a portrait or silhouette filled with a second picture: a forest, a city, clouds, or water. It is one of the fastest ways to make something that looks like real art.
The secret is blending modes: a Photoshop setting that changes how one layer mixes with the layer underneath it. Today you will stack two images and use blending modes to fuse them.

HOW TO BLEND / 03 / 04
Make Your Own
Here is the quick version. Follow the videos for the full details, then build your own.
Step 01
Open Your Base
Open your first photo. A portrait or a clean silhouette works best. This is your bottom layer.
Step 02
Add a Second Image
Place a second photo on top as a new layer: a landscape, a sky, trees, or a texture.
Step 03
Change the Blend Mode
In the Layers panel, change the top layer’s blend mode. Try Screen or Lighten to drop the dark areas, or Multiply to drop the light.
Step 04
Mask and Move
Add a layer mask and paint with black to hide parts you do not want. Move and resize the top image until it sits right.
Step 05
Adjust to Finish
Tweak brightness, contrast, and color so the two photos read as one image.

BLENDING MODES
Screen, Lighten, Multiply
Blending modes control how layers mix. Screen and Lighten keep the bright parts of the top layer; Multiply keeps the dark parts. Cycle through them and watch what happens, there is no wrong answer.
TURN IT IN / 04 / 04
Export and Submit
When your double exposure looks good, flatten it with Layer > Flatten Image, then export a JPG with File > Save a Copy.
Submit your JPG to this assignment in Canvas. Name it: FirstName LastInitial - Double Exposure.jpg
Experiment and have fun. There is no single right look, so try a few image combinations and pick your favorite.
