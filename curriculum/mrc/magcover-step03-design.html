Magazine Cover  •  Step 3
Build Your Cover
Design it like the real thing.
SET UP YOUR COVER / 01 / 05
Open the Template
Open the Magazine Cover template from your project folder with File > Open. It is already built for you: tall (portrait), 8.5 by 11 inches, RGB color, 300 ppi. You do not need to make your own.
You will see cyan guide lines a little in from each edge. These lines do not print. They mark the text-safe area: keep your masthead, headlines, cover lines, and anything important inside the lines so nothing gets trimmed off.
Your cover photo must full bleed. Open your best hero photo of Teddy through Camera Raw, place it, and extend it past the cyan lines all the way to the edges of the page, with no white border. Covers are cut at the edge, so the photo has to run right off the page.
LOCK THEM
Lock Your Guides
So you do not move the guides by accident, go to View > Lock Guides. They will stay put while you work.
Save your work as a .PSD in your project folder.
ADD THE MASTHEAD / 02 / 05
Turn On Your Logo Layer
All three magazine logos are already in the template as layers. In the Layers panel, find the logo for the magazine you chose and turn its layer on (click the eye icon). Hide the other two.
The masthead is the brand, so it sits big and bold at the top of your cover. Keep it inside the cyan guides.
ADD YOUR WORDS / 03 / 05
Title, Subtitle, and Breakout Points
Use the Type tool to add all the words from your interview. Scroll through each pointer.
Words 01
Main Title
One big, bold headline about Teddy is the star, and the largest text on the cover. Pull it from her brief, short and powerful, like “The Heartbeat of MRC.”
Words 02
Subtitle
Add a short line under the title (the deck) that gives one more detail about Teddy and her honor.
Words 03
Breakout Points
Add at least three smaller cover lines that tease other campus stories (the Ag Farm, livestock, Robotics), using facts from the brief or what you saw on campus.
Words 04
Use Type Hierarchy
Make Teddy’s title the biggest, the subtitle smaller, and the breakout points smaller still. Size shows what matters most.
Words 05
Keep Text Off the Face
Place your words in the open space you left when you shot. Never cover Teddy’s face or eyes.
Words 06
Match the Magazine
Use fonts and colors close to your real magazine’s style so your cover looks authentic.

ADD TWO BREAKOUT BOXES / 04 / 05
Feature Two More Stories
Add your two breakout boxes. Each one is a small box with one of your breakout photos inside and a few words next to it, like a mini cover line.
Keep them the same size and lined up neatly so they look clean and on purpose, just like the breakout boxes on a real cover.
SAVE AND SUBMIT / 05 / 05
Save as JPG and Submit
When your cover is done, flatten it with Layer > Flatten Image. Keep a PSD only if you want one.
Turn in a JPG, not a PSD. Use File > Save a Copy, choose JPG, full size. Name it:
FirstName LastInitial - Magazine Cover.jpg
Submit your finished cover JPG to Step 3 in Canvas.
