SET UP YOUR COVER / 01 / 04
Start a New File
Open Photoshop. Go to File > New and make a tall (portrait) magazine page, about 8.5 by 11 inches, RGB color, 300 ppi.
Open your best hero photo through Camera Raw, place it on your page, and let it bleed all the way to the edges with no white border. Save your work as a .PSD in your project folder.
ADD THE MASTHEAD / 02 / 04
Place the Logo
Open the magazine logo from your project folder and place it at the top of your cover.
Size it big and bold, like the real magazine. The masthead is the brand, so it should be one of the first things you see.
WRITE YOUR COVER LINES / 03 / 04
Add Your Headlines
Use the Type tool to add your cover lines. Scroll through each pointer.
Line 01
Write a Main Headline
One big, bold cover line is the star. Make it short and exciting, like “Built at the MRC” or “Future Farmers.”
Line 02
Add Teasers
Add 2 or 3 smaller cover lines that hint at other stories. These are your teasers.
Line 03
Use Type Hierarchy
Make the main headline the biggest. Make the teasers smaller. Size shows the reader what matters most.
Line 04
Keep Text Off the Face
Place your words in the open space you left when you shot. Never cover your subject’s face or eyes.
Line 05
Match the Magazine
Use fonts and colors close to your real magazine’s style so your cover looks authentic.

SAVE AND SUBMIT / 04 / 04
Save as JPG and Submit
When your cover is done, flatten it with Layer > Flatten Image. Keep a PSD only if you want one.
Turn in a JPG, not a PSD. Use File > Save a Copy, choose JPG, full size. Name it:
FirstName LastInitial - Magazine Cover.jpg
Submit your finished cover JPG to Step 3 in Canvas.
