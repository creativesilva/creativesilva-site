Photo Scavenger Hunt
Fifteen shots. Best set wins the prize.
THE MISSION / 01 / 05
Your First Photo Hunt
Today you grab a real camera and go hunting, not for animals, but for great shots. The Mark Richardson Center is full of them: livestock and crops, big machines, rusted metal, rough wood, and an American flag.
Here is the twist: this is a contest. Everyone shoots the same 15 shots, and the best set wins a prize. You do not need to know every button. You just need to look closely and frame each shot with care. This is how every photographer starts: by learning to see.
THE PRIZE / 02 / 05
Win a $20 Chick-fil-A Gift Card
The student with the best set of 15 photos wins a $20 Chick-fil-A gift card. One winner, chosen by Mr. Silva.
Every shot counts, so give each one your best effort. A strong, complete set beats a few lucky photos.
THE PRIZE
$20 Chick-fil-A Gift Card
The best full set of 15 photos takes it home. Make every shot count.
HOW IT WORKS / 03 / 05
The Same 15 Shots for Everyone
You get a paper shot list with 15 shots. Everyone shoots the same list, so it is a fair contest. Some are things to find, like an American flag or a tractor. Some are shot challenges, like leading lines or texture.
Take lots of photos as you go. Shoot each item more than once to get it right. Later you will pick your single best photo for each of the 15.
THE GOAL
Quality Over Speed
It is not a race. One well-framed photo beats five quick snapshots. Slow down and look before you press the shutter.
WORDS TO KNOW / 04 / 05
Photo Vocabulary
Learn these six terms. You will use them all over the hunt.
Vocabulary
Composition
How you arrange the things in your photo inside the frame.
Vocabulary
Leading Lines
Lines in a scene, like a road or fence, that pull the eye toward your subject.
Vocabulary
Rule of Thirds
Placing your subject off-center for a more natural, balanced shot.
Vocabulary
Framing
Shooting through something, like a gate or branches, to frame your subject.
Vocabulary
Fill the Frame
Getting close so your subject fills the photo, with no wasted empty space.
Vocabulary
Culling
Choosing your single best photo and dropping the rest.
