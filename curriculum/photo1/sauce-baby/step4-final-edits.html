Step 4 of 5
Final Edits: Crop, Edit, and Deliver
Select your 4 best images, crop them for Instagram, edit in Lightroom, and export client-ready JPEGs.
Your Assignment
Create 4 Client-Ready Images for Sauce Baby
Select your 4 strongest shots from the session. You will crop them to vertical 16:9 for Instagram, edit them in Lightroom Classic, and export 4 final JPEG files ready to show the client.
All editing can be done entirely in Lightroom. If you want to push your work further and get more creative, you can take individual images into Photoshop for advanced retouching and layered edits. That is optional and described in its own section below.
Submit: 4 final JPG images, vertical 9:16, 1080 × 1920 px, sRGB color space.
Before You Start
Review the Creative Brief and Instagram Format
Before you edit a single image, open the Sauce Baby Creative Brief and review every required shot. Your 4 final images must each match one of the required shot types. If anything is unclear, ask before you start editing. Study the format reference below. Your images must match it.
Sauce Baby Instagram sharing format reference. Study this before editing. Each of your 4 images must match one of these required shot types.
Step 1
Crop to 9×16 in Lightroom Classic
All 4 images must be cropped to vertical 9:16 before editing or exporting. This crop is non-destructive: your original RAW file is never changed.
Scroll to see all steps  →
01
Open Develop Module
Open your selected image in the Develop module in Lightroom Classic. All editing and crop tools live here.
02
Activate the Crop Tool
Press R on your keyboard or click the Crop icon in the toolbar below the histogram to activate the Crop tool.
03
Set Aspect to 16 / 9
Click the Aspect dropdown in the Crop panel and select 16 / 9. If it is not in the list, click Enter Custom and type 16 (Width) and 9 (Height), then click OK.
04
Lock the Ratio
Click the padlock icon to lock the ratio so it stays fixed while you adjust your composition.
05
Frame Your Shot
Drag the crop corners to frame your composition. Drag inside the box to reposition. Keep your product and key details inside the crop area.
06
Stay Vertical
Press X to toggle to portrait orientation if needed. All 4 images must stay in portrait (vertical) orientation.
07
Apply and Move On
Press Enter or click Done to apply the crop. Lightroom saves it non-destructively. Repeat for all 4 images.
Step 2
Edit in Lightroom Classic
Use the Develop module to build your edit. These are global tools that affect the whole image. This is where most of your editing work happens. Use as many or as few as your image needs.
Scroll to see all tools  →
Exposure
Adjust the overall brightness of the image. Start here before anything else.
Highlights & Shadows
Recover detail in bright areas and open up shadow areas without changing overall brightness.
White Balance
Adjust temperature and tint so your food colors look accurate, warm, and appetizing.
Tone Curve
Shape contrast and tonal range with precise control. Great for achieving a cinematic or editorial look.
HSL / Color
Adjust individual colors. Make reds more vibrant, cool down greens, or shift any color independently.
Color Grading
Apply creative color to shadows, midtones, and highlights for a branded, consistent visual mood.
Masking
Use Lightroom’s built-in masking tools to target adjustments to specific areas like the subject, background, or sky.
Sharpening
Sharpen fine product details and textures in the Detail panel so labels and surfaces look crisp.
Noise Reduction
Reduce grain in shadow areas, especially if you shot in low light or at a higher ISO.
OPTIONAL: LEVEL UP
Take It Further in Photoshop
You do not have to use Photoshop. Everything you need to submit strong final images can be done entirely in Lightroom. However, if you want to push your work to a higher level, get more creative, and produce something that feels truly polished and commercial, Photoshop gives you tools that Lightroom cannot. Detailed retouching, layered color work, pixel-level precision: that is what Photoshop adds. Students who choose to use it will have the opportunity to produce stronger, more competitive work.
If you choose to go this route, follow the three sections below in order.
Open in Photoshop: The Roundtrip Workflow
Scroll to see all steps  →
01
Select Your Image
In Lightroom Classic, select the image you want to open. You can be in the Library or Develop module.
02
Right-Click > Edit In
Right-click the image and choose Edit In > Edit in Adobe Photoshop. Or use the menu: Photo > Edit In > Edit in Adobe Photoshop.
03
Choose “Edit a Copy”
If a dialog appears, choose Edit a Copy with Lightroom Classic Adjustments. This sends a copy with your LR edits applied. Your RAW file stays safe.
04
Keep Lightroom Open
Do not close Lightroom while working in Photoshop. Both apps must stay open for the roundtrip to work correctly.
Advanced Photoshop Tools
Scroll to see all tools  →
Spot Healing Brush (J)
Remove blemishes, sauce drips, crumbs, dust, or surface imperfections with one click.
Clone Stamp (S)
Copy exact pixels from a clean area and paint them over a problem area you need to replace.
Adjustment Layers
Add Curves, Hue/Saturation, or Selective Color layers for deep non-destructive color control.
Layer Masks
Apply any adjustment to one specific area only by painting on a mask. Targeted, precise editing.
Dodge & Burn (O)
Selectively brighten or darken specific areas to add depth and a polished commercial quality.
Curves Layer
Precise tonal and color control across shadows, midtones, and highlights in individual RGB channels.
Liquify Filter
Filter > Liquify to subtly reshape elements. Use carefully and sparingly for small refinements only.
Save Back to Lightroom
Scroll to see all steps  →
01
File > Save Only
File > Save (Cmd+S / Ctrl+S). Do NOT use Save As or Export. Those break the link and the file will not return to Lightroom automatically.
02
Close the Document
After saving, close the image tab in Photoshop (File > Close or click the X on the tab). Closing is what triggers the file to return.
03
Check Lightroom
Switch back to Lightroom. Your edited file appears as a TIFF stacked next to the original. A stack icon shows on the thumbnail.
04
Repeat for Each Image
Repeat the full roundtrip for each image that needs Photoshop work. Then use the TIFF files when you export your final JPEGs.
Step 3
Export Your Final JPEG Files from Lightroom
Use these exact settings for all 4 exports. If you used Photoshop, export from your returned TIFF files. If you stayed in Lightroom, export from the edited RAW. Either way, the export settings are the same.
Scroll to see all steps  →
01
Open Export Dialog
Select your file and go to File > Export. (Mac: Shift+Cmd+E  /  PC: Shift+Ctrl+E)
02
Set Export Location
Choose your Final Edits folder inside your Commercial Photography Final project folder in OneDrive.
03
Format & Quality
Format: JPEG
Quality: 100
Color Space: sRGB
Instagram uses sRGB. Any other color space causes color shifts on upload.
04
Image Sizing
Check Resize to Fit. Choose Width and Height. Set W: 1080 px and H: 1920 px. True 9×16 at full Instagram resolution.
05
File Naming
Name files clearly:
YourName_SauceBaby_Final_01.jpg
Continue through 02, 03, and 04.
06
Export & Final Check
Click Export. Then open each image and confirm it matches one of the 4 required shots from the Creative Brief before submitting.

Referencia del formato de Instagram de Sauce Baby. Estudia esto antes de editar. Cada una de tus 4 imágenes debe coincidir con uno de estos tipos de toma requeridos.
Paso 1
Recorta a 9×16 en Lightroom Classic
Las 4 imágenes deben recortarse al formato vertical 9:16 antes de editar o exportar. Este recorte es no destructivo: tu archivo RAW original nunca cambia.
Desliza para ver todos los pasos  →
01
Abre Módulo Revelar
Abre tu imagen seleccionada en el módulo Revelar en Lightroom Classic. Todas las herramientas de edición y recorte están aquí.
02
Activa Herramienta Recorte
Presiona R en el teclado o haz clic en el ícono de recorte en la barra de herramientas debajo del histograma.
03
Configura Aspecto 16 / 9
Haz clic en el menú Aspecto y selecciona 16 / 9. Si no aparece, haz clic en Ingresar personalizado y escribe 16 (Ancho) y 9 (Alto), luego OK.
04
Bloquea la Proporción
Haz clic en el ícono del candado para bloquear la proporción y que no cambie mientras ajustas el encuadre.
05
Encuadra Tu Toma
Arrastra las esquinas del recuadro para componer. Arrastra dentro del recuadro para reposicionar. Mantén el producto y detalles clave dentro del área.
06
Mantén la Vertical
Presiona X para cambiar a orientación vertical si es necesario. Todas las imágenes deben estar en orientación vertical (retrato).
07
Aplica y Continúa
Presiona Enter o haz clic en Listo para aplicar el recorte. Lightroom lo guarda sin destruir. Repite para las 4 imágenes.
Paso 2
Edita en Lightroom Classic
Usa el módulo Revelar para construir tu edición. Estas herramientas globales afectan toda la imagen. Aquí ocurre la mayor parte de tu trabajo de edición. Usa las que tu imagen necesite.
Desliza para ver todas las herramientas  →
Exposición
Ajusta el brillo general de la imagen. Empieza aquí antes que con cualquier otra herramienta.
Iluminaciones y Sombras
Recupera detalles en áreas brillantes y abre las zonas oscuras sin cambiar el brillo general.
Balance de Blancos
Ajusta temperatura y tinte para que los colores de los alimentos se vean precisos, cálidos y apetitosos.
Curva de Tono
Da forma al contraste y rango tonal con control preciso. Excelente para un look cinemático o editorial.
HSL / Color
Ajusta colores individuales. Haz los rojos más vibrantes, enfría los verdes o cambia cualquier color de forma independiente.
Gradación de Color
Aplica color creativo a sombras, tonos medios e iluminaciones para un estado de ánimo visual consistente y con marca.
Máscaras
Usa las herramientas de máscara integradas de Lightroom para dirigir ajustes a áreas específicas como el sujeto o el fondo.
Enfoque
Enfoca detalles finos y texturas del producto en el panel Detalle para que etiquetas y superficies se vean nítidas.
Reducción de Ruido
Reduce el grano en las áreas de sombra, especialmente si fotografiaste con poca luz o con ISO elevado.
OPCIONAL: NIVEL AVANZADO
Lleva Tu Trabajo Más Lejos en Photoshop
No tienes que usar Photoshop. Todo lo que necesitas para entregar imágenes finales fuertes puede hacerse completamente en Lightroom. Sin embargo, si quieres llevar tu trabajo a un nivel más alto, ser más creativo y producir algo que se sienta verdaderamente pulido y comercial, Photoshop te da herramientas que Lightroom no puede. Retoques detallados, trabajo de color en capas, precisión a nivel de píxel: eso es lo que Photoshop agrega. Los estudiantes que elijan usarlo tendrán la oportunidad de producir un trabajo más fuerte y competitivo.
Si eliges este camino, sigue las tres secciones de abajo en orden.

Herramientas Avanzadas de Photoshop

Pincel Reparación Puntual (J)
Elimina manchas, gotas de salsa, migas, polvo o imperfecciones de superficie con un solo clic.
Sello de Clonar (S)
Copia píxeles exactos de un área limpia y píntalos sobre un área problemática que necesitas reemplazar.
Capas de Ajuste
Agrega Curvas, Tono/Saturación o capas de Color selectivo para control de color profundo y no destructivo.
Máscaras de Capa
Aplica cualquier ajuste solo a un área específica pintando sobre una máscara. Edición dirigida y precisa.
Subexponer y Sobreexponer (O)
Aclara u oscurece áreas selectivas para agregar profundidad y una calidad comercial pulida al producto.
Capa de Curvas
Control tonal y de color preciso en sombras, tonos medios e iluminaciones en canales RGB individuales.
Filtro Licuar
Filtro > Licuar para remodelar sutilmente elementos. Úsalo con cuidado y solo para pequeñas correcciones.
